Noise Characterization of Ultrasound Channel Data via Simulation-Based Inference

Simulation-Based Inference Noise Modeling Ultrasound Bayesian Inference Channel Data

Overview

Raw ultrasound channel data — recorded before any beamforming — contains complex, spatially and temporally correlated noise whose precise statistical structure is rarely known analytically. Accurate noise characterization is essential for designing optimal beamformers and evaluating image quality. This project applies simulation-based inference (SBI), a likelihood-free Bayesian framework, to infer the parameters of noise models directly from observed channel data.

Motivation

Traditional noise characterization assumes simple, tractable noise models (e.g. additive white Gaussian noise). In practice, ultrasound channel data is corrupted by clutter, electronic noise, reverberation, and off-axis scattering — combinations that resist closed-form likelihood evaluation. SBI bypasses this requirement, enabling inference even when the likelihood is intractable, by learning a surrogate from simulated data.

Key Contributions

  • Formulated noise characterization in ultrasound channel data as a Bayesian parameter estimation problem.
  • Built a forward simulator capturing relevant noise sources in the ultrasound acquisition chain.
  • Applied sequential neural posterior estimation (SNPE) to learn the posterior over noise parameters.
  • Validated inferred noise parameters against held-out experimental datasets.

Domain-Adaptive Deep Neural Network Beamformer for Echocardiography

Deep Learning Beamforming Echocardiography Domain Adaptation Medical Imaging

Overview

Conventional delay-and-sum (DAS) beamforming in echocardiography produces images with limited contrast and resolution. Deep neural network (DNN) beamformers offer a powerful alternative, but their performance often degrades when deployed on data from acquisition settings or equipment not seen during training — a problem known as domain shift. This project addresses that gap by developing a domain-adaptive DNN beamformer that maintains high image quality across varied clinical echocardiography domains.

Motivation

Echocardiography is the primary imaging modality for assessing cardiac structure and function, yet image quality is highly sensitive to probe frequency, patient anatomy, and acquisition protocol. A beamformer trained on one clinical setting may fail silently on another, limiting real-world utility. Domain adaptation provides a principled path to robust, generalizable models.

Key Contributions

  • Designed a DNN beamforming architecture operating directly on raw channel data.
  • Incorporated domain adaptation techniques to reduce the covariate shift between training and target acquisition domains.
  • Evaluated image quality metrics (contrast ratio, generalized contrast-to-noise ratio) across multiple echocardiographic datasets.
  • Demonstrated improved robustness compared to standard DAS and unadapted DNN baselines.
